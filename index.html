Blue Worm Vehicle

Arduino SolidWorks Spring 2025

The Concept

Maintenance within industrial ventilation systems poses significant risks to human workers due to tight spaces and the potential for dangerous gas impurities. Sending humans into these environments is often costly and hazardous. The objective was to create a remote-controlled vehicle capable of navigating these confined, winding environments to measure CO2 levels, ensuring it is safe before any human intervention occurs. The main constraint was the tight turning radius required inside narrow ducts.

Key Features

I engineered a unique 'worm-joint' articulation system. Instead of standard wheels for steering, the vehicle bends its entire body to turn, allowing it to snake through sharp angles in narrow ducts.

  • Bluetooth Control: Utilized Arduino and HC-05 modules to drive servo motors for precise joint articulation remotely.
  • Safety Monitoring: Integrated real-time CO2 sensors to transmit air quality data back to the operator.
  • Rapid Prototyping: Designed custom chassis parts in SolidWorks and 3D printed them for iterative testing to ensure the joints could handle the torque.

ECG Monitoring System

Fall 2025 Analog Design MATLAB

Overview

Electrocardiogram (ECG) monitoring is critical for detecting heart irregularities. This project involved building the entire signal chain from scratch: capturing the raw bio-potential signal from the body using electrodes, filtering it through analog circuits, and finally digitizing and cleaning it using software algorithms.

Technical Implementation

I designed the analog front-end circuit to capture the millivolt-level signals and the digital back-end to make them readable.

  • Instrumentation amplifiers for weak signal boost.
  • Active High/Low pass filtering to remove DC offset and high-frequency noise.
  • MATLAB Notch filters for 60Hz interference removal.
