Blue Worm Vehicle

A Bluetooth-driven robot inspired by worm joints. Utilized Arduino and servo motors for navigation in narrow vents. Integrated a CO2 sensor for air quality safety.

See the robot in action:

Watch on YouTube

The Challenge

When working with ventilation systems, the level of gas impurities in the vents can be dangerous for workers to work with directly. This vehicle measures the CO2 level in the ventilations and drives through the tight angle utilizing a worm-inspired turning mechanism that runs on bluetooth.

The Solution

I designed a "worm-joint" articulation system that allows the vehicle to bend its body to turn, rather than relying on standard steering.

  • Engineered a Bluetooth-driven mechanism utilizing Arduino and servo motors to control the joint articulation.
  • Designed custom chassis parts in SolidWorks and 3D printed them for rapid prototyping.
  • Integrated a CO2 sensor to measure gas concentration in real-time, ensuring air quality remains within healthy limits.
