Blue Worm Vehicle

A Bluetooth-driven robot inspired by biological worm joints, designed to navigate narrow vents and monitor air safety.

Spring 2025
Role: Electrical Design
Arduino
SolidWorks

The Challenge

Maintenance within industrial ventilation systems poses significant risks to human workers due to tight spaces and the potential for dangerous gas impurities. Sending humans into these environments is often costly and hazardous.

The objective was to create a remote-controlled vehicle capable of navigating these confined, winding environments to measure CO2 levels, ensuring it is safe before any human intervention occurs. The main constraint was the tight turning radius required inside narrow ducts.

The Solution

I engineered a unique "worm-joint" articulation system. Instead of standard wheels for steering, the vehicle bends its entire body to turn, allowing it to snake through sharp angles in narrow ducts.

  • Bluetooth Control: Utilized Arduino and HC-05 modules to drive servo motors for precise joint articulation remotely.
  • Rapid Prototyping: Designed custom chassis parts in SolidWorks and 3D printed them for iterative testing to ensure the joints could handle the torque.
  • Safety Monitoring: Integrated real-time CO2 sensors to transmit air quality data back to the operator.
